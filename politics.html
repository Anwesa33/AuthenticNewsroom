Canine custody battle lands Mohua Moitra in a ‘cash-for-query’ row.

Kolkata, West Bengal

10th November, 2023 | 10:02pm

20k

Share:

Mahua Moitra, a Member of Parliament for Trinamool Congress (TMC) from West Bengal state's Krishnanagar constituency, was accused of accepting money and expensive gifts in exchange for asking certain questions in Lok Sabha. The row has been dubbed as a 'cash for query' controversy. She was also accused of sharing her parliamentary login data with unauthorised people.

The allegations were made by her former personal friend and lawyer Jai Anant Dehadrai and Nishikant Dubey — a lawmaker from the ruling party in central, Bharatiya Janata Party. A Mumbai-based businessman Darshan Hiranandani, with whom Moitra was accused of sharing her credentials, has accepted that he used them to question Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi's government on matters related to another businessman Gautam Adani. He also claims that these questions were asked to "malign and embarrass" Prime Minister Narendra Modi. He accuses Moitra of "made frequent demands" for "expensive luxury items, providing support on the renovation of her officially allotted bungalow in Delhi, travel expenses, holidays, etc, apart from providing secretarial and logistical help for her travels within India and to different parts of the world."

In the wake of these events, Mahua Moitra has filed a defamation suit before the Delhi High Court against Dehadrai and Dubey. Moitra has accepted that she shared her online credentials with a businessman friend to allow submission of her pre-approved questions so she can ask them later in Lok Sabha. It all started with a custodial battle over Henry, a canine whose ownership is being claimed by both Mahua Moitra and Jai Anant Dehadrai, Moitra's former personal friend. She referred to this charges against her as based on “jilted ex’s lies”Moitra also filed a number of police complaints against Dehadrai in the last six months for alleged criminal trespass, theft, vulgar messages and abuse.In the latest turn of events, Moitra has complained to Lok Sabha MP Om Birla that she is being "targeted for speaking out" against Adani Group, while referring to the details of the proceedings of Lok Sabha's Ethics Committee being reported in the media.

The Parliamentary Ethics Committee made of 10 members, that is looking into the cash-for-query allegations against Trianmool Congress MP Mahua Moitra, approved the recommendation of her expulsion from parliament.The panel has found her actions "highly objectionable, unethical, heinous and criminal".According to sources, six members had voted in favour of the recommendation and four had voted against it. The recommendations of the committee will be submitted to Lok Sabha Speaker Om Birla tomorrow.Ms Moitra had walked out of the Ethics Committee meeting held last week, accusing them of asking intrusive personal questions. Ethics panel chief Vinod Kumar Sonkar claimed that she is non-cooperative.
