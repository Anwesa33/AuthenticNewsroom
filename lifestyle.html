2024 sees revival of sarees, where tradition meets contemporary fashion

Bardhaman, West Bengal

4th May, 2024 | 12:30pm

12k

Share:

Sarees are an integral part of Indian cultural wear. This timeless and sustainable attire is regaining it’s rightful place in the hearts of Indian fashion enthusiasts with the recent fusion fashion trends. GenZ has a knack for sustainable and ethically sourced fashion, so local artisans and their partner brands are gaining popularity with them. Saree is not just confined to Indian fashion space anymore, it’s being appreciated by many international celebrities, many wearing them in auspicious red carpet events. Even the Met Gala is seeing saree-clad footfall. Alia Bhatt’s saree look at Met Gala 2024, designed by Sabyasachi Mukherjee was a beautiful sight.

Fusion fashion in saree is seen in all aspects of it, from the weave to the prints, colour pallets and accessories. Classic weaves like banarasi and kanjivaram are getting newer colour palettes. From auspicious red for weddings to black for elegance. Draping styles are changing too, mermaid drapes, and infinity drapes are quite popular among youngsters these days, and regional traditional draping styles are gaining popularity among people outside the region. Pairing a saree with shapewear instead of the petticoat, wearing it with jeans etc is becoming common. Blouses are often changed for shirts, blazers, regional upper body wear meant for other attires etc. Pastel is all the rage as a colour pallet worldwide for fashion, Indian designers are taking the bold move of diverging from that and delving into neon, black and red combinations. Black and red in every weave is making highlights.

Sarees these days are often accessorized differently from what we used to see. Dainty jewellery pieces with heavy weaves are in, and sneakers and boots are making handling the saree a breeze for everyone in style. Belts are a major accessory trend in 2024, from thin leather belts to corsets to bedazzled ones. Regional art forms are making bold appearances in sarees of celebrities. Zari works in weaves like kanjivaram are changing in style representing more trendy motifs. An exciting fusion of heritage and contemporary style is presented at Ethnic Plus in 2024 with a revival of traditional wedding sarees tailored specifically for the modern bride.

Sarees made of Banarasi Silk, Banglory Silk, Georgette, Jacquard, Net Organza, Satin, Silk Tussar, and Kanjivaram capture the imaginations of brides who seek elegance without compromising their individuality. The sarees continue to weave tales of tradition, love, and everlasting beauty on the stage of the modern wedding ceremony. Many small saree brands are printing sarees in various themes, making them quirky and fun to wear. Gen Z taking interest in this fusion style works is making saree everyday wear. The discourse of sustainable fashion is giving the fusion saree trend a boost. The saree’s versatility and longevity, emotional value for many women make the saree a more obvious choices for fashion enthusiasts in India and worldwide.
